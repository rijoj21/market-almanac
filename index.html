§ 03

Field Notes

A few ideas that confuse almost everyone at first. Here they are in plain language.

A.

Index Fund — the lazy genius

Instead of betting on a few companies, an index fund buys a tiny slice of all of them in a list (an "index" like the S&P 500 or Nifty 50). You're not trying to beat the market — you're quietly riding it. Low effort, low fees, broad spread.

B.

SIP vs. Lump Sum — drip or splash

A lump sum drops all your money in at once. A SIP spreads it out, a little each month, so you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when high — averaging out the bumps. SIPs suit a salary; lump sums suit a windfall.

C.

Trading vs. Investing — sprint vs. marathon

Trading chases short-term price moves — days, minutes, seconds. Investing plants money and waits years for it to grow. Both use the same markets but different clocks, and they reward very different temperaments.

D.

Risk & Return — the eternal trade

Higher potential returns almost always come with a higher chance of loss. There is no free lunch — anything promising big gains with zero risk is a story, not an investment. Understanding your own comfort with swings is half the game.

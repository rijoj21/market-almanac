Plate IV · The raw materials beneath the markets

Commodities — the
stuff the world runs on.

Stocks are slices of companies. Commodities are the raw materials themselves — gold, oil, copper, wheat. Their prices are set by supply and demand, weather, and world events, which makes them a very different market to understand.

How commodities differ from stocks

When you buy a share, you own part of a business that can grow, earn and pay dividends. A commodity earns nothing — a bar of gold is the same bar in ten years. You're betting purely on its price, driven by how much exists and how much the world wants. That's why many people hold a little as a hedge: commodities often zig when stocks zag.

Supply & demand
A drought, a mine closure or an OPEC decision can move a price overnight.
No earnings
Commodities don't grow or pay you — the only return is a change in price.
A different rhythm
They often rise when shares fall, which is why they're used to diversify.

Popular ETFs & indices

§ C

How money has grown — a historical illustration

A fair question for any learner is "what might I earn?" Nobody can answer that for the future — so instead this shows how a one-time investment would have grown at each market's long-run historical average. It is a teaching illustration, not a forecast.

Why the rates differ: mature markets like the US and Europe have long, steady histories; faster-growing markets like India have historically shown higher average returns — but typically with bigger swings along the way. Higher historical return almost always rode alongside higher risk. That trade-off is the whole lesson.

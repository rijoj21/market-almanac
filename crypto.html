Cryptocurrency — money
made of code.

Crypto assets live on blockchains — shared public ledgers no single company controls. Unlike a stock, most have no business or profit behind them; their price comes purely from supply, demand and belief. That makes them fascinating to learn about — and extraordinarily risky.

⚠️

Understand the risk: crypto prices can double or halve in weeks, and coins can fall to zero. The market is down sharply so far in 2026. Never invest money you can't afford to lose — this page is for learning the vocabulary only, not a suggestion to buy anything.

How crypto differs from stocks

A share is a piece of a company that can earn money and pay dividends. Most crypto tokens represent a network or an idea instead — there's no profit underneath, so the only return is a change in price. Some coins aim to be money (Bitcoin), some are platforms for apps (Ethereum), and some simply track the dollar (stablecoins). Knowing which is which is the whole first lesson.

Usually no company
Most tokens are a network or community, not a business with earnings.
Extremely volatile
Double-digit moves in a day are normal; total loss is genuinely possible.
Always on
Crypto trades 24/7 worldwide — no opening bell, no closing bell.
